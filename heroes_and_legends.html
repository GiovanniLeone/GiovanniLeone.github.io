Randy Shughart and Gary Gordon

Randy Shughart and Gary Gordon are recognized for their courageous attempt to protect the crashed helicopter "Super Six Four" during Operation Gothic Serpent in Somolia. Both of them knew they were not coming back from their decision. Because of their actions, both Randy Shughart and Gary Gordon recieved the Medal of Honor.

Meriwether Lewis and William Clark

Meriwether Lewis and William Clark were American explorers that traveled through the American interior to the Pacific Northwest in 1804-1806 after the Louisiana purchase made by Thomas Jefferson. Lewis and Clark were courageous enough to traverse through the unknown, dangerous terrain that spanned 8000 miles.

Desmond Doss

Desmond Doss was a company aid man when the 1st Battalion attacked a jagged escarpment held by the Japanese. On May 21 during the night attack near Shuri, Doss remained exposed to gunfire and artillery healing the wounded while the rest of his company took cover. Through his outstanding bravery and determination, Doss saved the lives of 75 soldiers and became a symbol throughout the 77th Infantry Division.

John Rockefeller

John D. Rockefeller was not a afraid of hard work. As a teenager, he embarked on a number of small business ventures. By the age of 20, Rockefeller ventured out to the business world with a business partner. In the early 1860's, Rockefeller sees how broken the oil refining industry is and decides it would be a good business move to open up his first refinery in 1863. Rockefeller decides to go full time in the oil business. In 1870, Rockefeller incorporated his Standard Oil Company. Innovations were made in the oil industry. One of them being the transportation of oil in pipelines. Standard oil soon to control about 90% of the oil market share, fueling automobiles and creating light for homes all over the United States.
